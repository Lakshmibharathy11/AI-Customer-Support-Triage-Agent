# Fixing SDK-1042 schema validation errors

## Introduction to SDK-1042 Errors
The SDK-1042 error code indicates an event schema validation failure in Stacklytics. This error occurs when the data sent to our API via one of our SDKs (available in JavaScript, Python, or Go) does not match the expected format. To resolve this issue, you'll need to review your event data and ensure it aligns with our schema requirements. You can find more information on our API and SDKs at the [API base URL](https://api.stacklytics.io/v2).

## Troubleshooting SDK-1042 Errors
To troubleshoot the SDK-1042 error, start by checking your event data for any formatting issues or missing required fields. You can also refer to our [status page](https://status.stacklytics.io) to see if there are any known issues that may be contributing to the error. If you're still having trouble, you can reach out to our support team for assistance. As a Gold, Diamond, or Platinum tier customer, you can expect a support response within 8 hours, 4 hours, or 1 hour, respectively, Monday through Friday, 9am-5pm Pacific Time.

## Resolving and Preventing SDK-1042 Errors
Once you've identified the issue with your event data, you can make the necessary corrections and retry sending the data to our API. To prevent future SDK-1042 errors, make sure to regularly review your event data and schema to ensure they remain aligned. If you have any questions or need further assistance, don't hesitate to reach out to our support team. Keep in mind that if you're experiencing frequent errors, it may be worth considering an upgrade to a higher tier, such as Diamond or Platinum, for additional support and features, including priority support response times and dedicated customer success managers.
