# Creating your first dashboard

## Introduction to Dashboards
To get the most out of Stacklytics, creating a dashboard is a great first step. As a Gold, Diamond, or Platinum tier customer, you have access to a range of features that can help you visualize your product's performance. If you're new to Stacklytics, start by checking the status page at https://status.stacklytics.io to ensure there are no ongoing issues. You can then begin setting up your dashboard to track key metrics and events.

## Setting up Your Dashboard
When setting up your dashboard, make sure to use a valid API key to avoid a 401 error, which indicates an invalid API key. You can use our JavaScript, Python, or Go SDKs to integrate with our API, which is located at https://api.stacklytics.io/v2. Be mindful of the rate limit to avoid a 429 error, which means you've exceeded the rate limit. If you encounter an SDK-1042 error, it means there's an issue with your event schema validation.

## Tips and Best Practices
As you create your dashboard, keep in mind the features and limits of your tier. For example, Gold tier customers have a limit of 1M events per month, while Diamond and Platinum tier customers have higher or unlimited event limits. If you need to upgrade or have questions about your tier, you can reach out to our support team, who will respond within 8 hours for Gold tier customers, 4 hours for Diamond tier customers, and 1 hour for Platinum tier customers. By following these tips and best practices, you'll be able to create a dashboard that helps you get the most out of Stacklytics and drive insights for your product.
