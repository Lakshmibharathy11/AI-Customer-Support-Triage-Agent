# Managing team roles and permissions

## Introduction to Team Management
Stacklytics allows teams to collaborate effectively by managing roles and permissions. As a team administrator, you can assign different roles to your team members based on their responsibilities. The number of seats available for your team depends on your subscription tier, with Gold including 1 seat, Diamond including up to 10 seats, and Platinum offering unlimited seats. You can find more information about your current plan and available seats by logging into your account.

## Assigning Roles and Permissions
To assign roles and permissions, you need to log in to your Stacklytics account and navigate to the team management section. From there, you can add new team members and assign them specific roles, such as administrator, developer, or viewer. Each role has its own set of permissions, and you can customize these permissions to fit your team's needs. If you encounter any issues during this process, you can refer to our API documentation at https://api.stacklytics.io/v2 or contact our support team, who will respond within 8 hours for Gold tier, 4 hours for Diamond tier, and 1 hour for Platinum tier.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues
When managing team roles and permissions, you may encounter common errors such as SDK-1042, which indicates an event schema validation failure. You can resolve this issue by checking your event schema and ensuring it aligns with our API requirements. If you experience any other issues, you can check our status page at https://status.stacklytics.io for any ongoing maintenance or outages. Additionally, you can refer to our documentation on common errors, such as 401 for invalid API keys and 429 for rate limit exceeded, to troubleshoot and resolve the issues quickly.
