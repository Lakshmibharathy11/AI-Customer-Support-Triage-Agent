# Setting up SSO / SAML (Platinum)

## Introduction to SSO/SAML
As a Platinum customer, you have access to Single Sign-On (SSO) and Security Assertion Markup Language (SAML) features, allowing you to manage user authentication and authorization in a more secure and efficient way. This feature is included in your Platinum plan, which also offers unlimited seats, unlimited events, and a dedicated Customer Success Manager (CSM). To get started with SSO/SAML, you will need to configure your identity provider to work with Stacklytics. You can find more information about our plans and features on our website.

## Configuring SSO/SAML
To configure SSO/SAML, you will need to provide your identity provider's metadata to Stacklytics, which will then be used to establish a secure connection between your systems. You can find the necessary configuration settings in your Stacklytics account, under the security settings section. If you encounter any issues during the setup process, you can refer to our API documentation at https://api.stacklytics.io/v2 or contact our support team, who will respond within 1 hour, as per our Platinum support SLA.

## Troubleshooting and Support
If you encounter any errors during the SSO/SAML setup process, you can check our common error codes for troubleshooting, such as the 401 error for invalid API keys or the 429 error for rate limit exceeded (error code RATE_LIMIT_EXCEEDED). You can also check our status page at https://status.stacklytics.io for any ongoing issues or maintenance. If you need further assistance, you can contact our support team, who will be happy to help you resolve any issues and ensure that you are getting the most out of your Platinum plan.
