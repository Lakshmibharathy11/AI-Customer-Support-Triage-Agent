# Updating your payment method

## Introduction to Payment Method Updates
To ensure uninterrupted access to Stacklytics, it's essential to keep your payment method up to date. If your payment method is invalid or expired, you may encounter issues with your account, such as delayed or missed invoices. As a Stacklytics customer, you can update your payment method at any time to avoid any disruptions to your service. This is particularly important for customers on the Gold tier, who are auto-charged every month.

## Updating Your Payment Method
To update your payment method, simply log in to your Stacklytics account and navigate to the billing section. From there, you can add a new payment method or update an existing one. If you're experiencing issues with your payment method, such as a declined payment, you may receive an error message or notice from our system. In some cases, you may also encounter error codes like 401, which indicates an invalid API key, but this is unrelated to payment method updates.

## Resolving Payment-Related Issues
If you're having trouble updating your payment method or have questions about your invoice, you can submit a ticket to our support team. For billing disputes, be sure to tag your ticket with "billing-dispute" and submit it within 14 days of the invoice date. Our support team is available Monday-Friday, 9am-5pm Pacific Time, and response times vary depending on your tier, from 1 hour for Platinum customers to 8 hours for Gold customers. You can also check the status of our system on our status page at https://status.stacklytics.io to ensure there are no ongoing issues that may be affecting your account.
