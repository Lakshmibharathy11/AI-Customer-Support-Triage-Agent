# Upgrading or downgrading your plan

## Introduction to Plan Changes
Upgrading or downgrading your plan in Stacklytics can be done to accommodate the changing needs of your engineering team. Whether you're looking to increase your event limit or require more advanced support, our plans are designed to be flexible. The Gold, Diamond, and Platinum tiers offer varying levels of support, event limits, and features. If you're experiencing issues with your current plan, such as hitting the rate limit (error code 429, RATE_LIMIT_EXCEEDED), it may be time to consider an upgrade.

## Plan Comparison
The following table highlights the key differences between our plans:
| Tier | Seats | Events per Month | Support Response Time | Payment Terms |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Gold | 1 | 1M | 8 hours | Net-15, auto-charged |
| Diamond | up to 10 | 10M | 4 hours | Net-15, invoiced |
| Platinum | unlimited | unlimited | 1 hour | Net-30, invoiced |

## Downgrade and Refund Policies
If you decide to downgrade your plan, you'll receive a prorated credit for the remaining days in your billing cycle. Additionally, if you're on an annual plan, you can take advantage of our 30-day money-back guarantee. Please note that any disputes regarding your invoice must be submitted as a ticket tagged "billing-dispute" within 14 days of the invoice date. You can find more information about our billing and refund policies on our website, and check the status of our services at https://status.stacklytics.io.
