# Understanding your invoice

## Introduction to Invoicing
Invoices for Stacklytics are issued on the 1st of each month. If you have any disputes regarding your invoice, you must submit a ticket tagged "billing-dispute" within 14 days of the invoice date. This ensures that any issues are addressed promptly and efficiently. It's essential to review your invoice carefully to avoid any potential disputes.

## Billing Tiers and Limits
The following table provides an overview of the billing tiers and their respective limits:
| Tier | Payment Terms | Seats | Events per Month |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Gold | Net-15, auto-charged | 1 | 1M |
| Diamond | Net-15, invoiced | up to 10 | 10M |
| Platinum | Net-30, invoiced | unlimited | unlimited |
This table highlights the key differences between the Gold, Diamond, and Platinum tiers, including payment terms, number of seats, and events per month.

## Resolving Billing Disputes and Refunds
If you've submitted a billing dispute, our support team will review your ticket and respond accordingly. Additionally, Stacklytics offers a 30-day money-back guarantee on annual plans, and downgrades result in prorated credits. For any questions or concerns regarding your invoice or billing, you can contact our support team, who will respond within the specified support hours (Monday-Friday, 9am-5pm Pacific Time) and according to your tier's support response time (8 hours for Gold, 4 hours for Diamond, and 1 hour for Platinum).
