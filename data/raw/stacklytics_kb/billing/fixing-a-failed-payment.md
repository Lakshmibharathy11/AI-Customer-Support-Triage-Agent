# Fixing a failed payment

## Introduction to Payment Issues
If you're experiencing a failed payment, it's likely due to an issue with your payment method or billing information. As a Stacklytics customer, you can review your billing details and payment history by logging into your account. If you're on the Gold tier, note that your payment is auto-charged, so it's essential to ensure your payment method is up-to-date. You can check your account settings to verify your payment information.

## Resolving Payment Failures
To resolve a failed payment, first, check your email for any notifications from Stacklytics regarding the issue. If you've received an error message, review it carefully to identify the cause of the problem. Common issues include invalid API keys (error code 401) or rate limit exceeded errors (error code 429 or RATE_LIMIT_EXCEEDED), but these are typically related to API usage rather than payment. If you're unable to resolve the issue on your own, you can submit a ticket to our support team, who will respond within the timeframe specified by your tier (8 hours for Gold, 4 hours for Diamond, or 1 hour for Platinum).

## Preventing Future Payment Issues
To prevent future payment issues, ensure that your billing information is accurate and up-to-date. If you've recently updated your payment method, allow some time for the changes to take effect. Additionally, be aware of our billing cycle, as invoices are issued on the 1st of each month. If you have any disputes regarding your invoice, be sure to submit a ticket tagged "billing-dispute" within 14 days of the invoice date to request a review of your bill.
