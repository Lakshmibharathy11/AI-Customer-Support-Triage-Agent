# Known issues and workarounds

## Introduction to Known Issues
Stacklytics is a reliable B2B product-analytics SaaS, but like any complex system, it's not immune to issues. Our team is dedicated to resolving problems quickly and providing workarounds when necessary. If you're experiencing difficulties, check the [status page](https://status.stacklytics.io) for any ongoing incidents. Common errors include the 401 error for invalid API keys and the 429 error (RATE_LIMIT_EXCEEDED) for exceeding rate limits.

## Error Codes and Solutions
When using our API (base URL: https://api.stacklytics.io/v2) or SDKs (available in JavaScript, Python, and Go), you may encounter specific error codes. For instance, the 401 error indicates an invalid API key, which can be resolved by verifying and updating your API key. The SDK-1042 error is related to event schema validation failure, and our support team can assist with troubleshooting. If you're experiencing rate limit issues (429 error), consider optimizing your API requests or upgrading to a higher tier, such as Diamond or Platinum, for increased event limits.

## Troubleshooting and Support
If you're unable to resolve an issue on your own, our support team is available to help. As a Gold, Diamond, or Platinum customer, you can expect standard support response times of 8 hours, 4 hours, or 1 hour, respectively, Monday through Friday, 9am-5pm Pacific Time. For urgent matters, Platinum customers can also expect escalations to be reviewed within 2 business hours. When submitting a support ticket, be sure to include any relevant error codes or details to help our team assist you efficiently.
